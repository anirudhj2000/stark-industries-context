Tokenization

Text is broken into tokens—subword units that the model can understand. Type something below to see tokenization in action!

Why Subword Tokenization?

Instead of treating each word or character separately, LLMs use subword tokenization (like BPE or WordPiece). This balances vocabulary size with the ability to handle rare words by breaking them into known pieces. For example, "unbelievable" might become ["un", "believ", "able"].

Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)

The base model is trained on high-quality demonstration data—examples of desired behavior written by humans. This teaches the model to follow instructions and produce helpful responses.

SLIDE 05 / 05

RLHF

Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback aligns the model with human preferences. Try it yourself—which response is better?

Response A
The capital of France is Paris. It's located in the north-central part of the country and has been the capital since the 10th century.
Response B
Paris is the capital. It's a city in Europe where people live and there are buildings. The Eiffel Tower is tall.
✨

Great choice!

Human feedback like this is collected at scale to train a reward model, which then guides the LLM to produce better responses through reinforcement learning.
